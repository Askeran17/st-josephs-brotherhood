Information for pilgrims


In order to get to Holy Mount Athos, where the holy elder Joseph and his disciples labored, you need to have a Greek visa and a special entry permit - the so-called diamonitirion, that is, a pass to Athos. The number of visitors to Mount Athos daily is limited - 120 people with a general pass and an unlimited number with a private one, but there are not many of these. It is best to order a pass 1-2 months before the intended trip. During the summer months there are more pilgrims and tourists, so you need to book your pass in advance. The maximum time for ordering is 6 months before the trip. Most often, monasteries do not refuse requests to visit Athos. However, if this is the main purpose of the trip, then you should buy a ticket and apply for a visa only after you receive a notification from the relevant monastery or from the Pilgrimage Bureau in Thessaloniki that entry to Athos on the specified day is permitted. Requests for pilgrimage to Mount Athos should be sent to the Pilgrimage Office of Mount Athos in Thessaloniki.


Address


Pilgrimage Bureau of Mount Athos in Thessaloniki. Thessaloniki, st. Egnatia, building 109. Tel +(30-2310) 252575.


Opening hours:


Monday-Friday from 8.30 to 14.00, Saturday from 10.00 to 12.00. Closed on religious and official holidays.
